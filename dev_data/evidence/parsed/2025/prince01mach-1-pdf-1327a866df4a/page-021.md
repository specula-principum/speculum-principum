For the Romans did in these cases what all
wise princes should do, who consider not only
present but also future discords and diligently
guard against them; for being foreseen they can
easily be remedied, but ifone waits till they are
at hand, the medicine is no longer in time as
the malady has become incurable; it happening
with this as with those hectic fevers, as doctors
say, which at their beginning are easy to cure
but difficult to recognise, but in course of time
when they have not at first been recognised and
treated, become easy to recognise and difficult
to cure. Thus it happens in matters of state; for
knowing afar off (which it is only given to a
prudent man to do) the evils that are brewing,
they are easily cured. But when, for want of
such knowledge, they are allowed to grow so that
