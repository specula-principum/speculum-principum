But when a prince acquires a new state as an
addition to his old one, then it is necessary to
disarm that state, except those who in acquiring
ithave sided with you; and even these one must,
when time and opportunity serve, render weak
and effeminate, and arrange things so that all
the arms of the new state are in the hands ofyour
soldiers who live near you in your old state.
Our forefathers and those who were esteemed
wise used to say that it was necessary to hold
Pistoia by means of factions and Pisa with fort-
resses, and for this purpose they fomented differ-
ences in some of their subject towns in order
to possess them more easily. In those days when
there was a balance of power in Italy, this
was doubtless well done, but does not seem to
me to be a good precept for the present time, for
I do not believe that the divisions thus created
ever do any good; on the contrary itis certain
that when the enemy approaches, the cities thus
divided will be at once lost, for the weaker fac-
tion will always side with the enemy and the
other will not be able to stand.
