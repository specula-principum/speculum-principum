.The duke being now powerful and partly se-
cured against present perils, being armed him-
self, and having in a great measure put down
those neighbouring forces which might injure
him, had now to get the respect of France, if he
wished to proceed with his acquisitions, for he
knew that the king, who had lately discovered
his error, would not give him any help. He began
therefore to seek fresh alliances and to vacillate
with France on the occasion of the expedi-
tion that the French were undertaking towards
the kingdom of Naples against the Spaniards,
who were besieging Gaeta. His intention was to
assure himself of them, which he would soon
have succeeded in doing ifAlexander had lived.
